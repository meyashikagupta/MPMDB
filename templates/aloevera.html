Aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis Mill.)

Aloe vera has been stewarded for centuries for its polysaccharide-rich gel that soothes, hydrates, and accelerates epithelial repair. Its vitamin- and mineral-dense profile has extended into topical, nutraceutical, and ingestible formats.

Botanical and nutritional snapshot

Aloe vera leaves accumulate a gel rich in vitamins A, C, and E alongside calcium, magnesium, zinc, selenium, and immunomodulatory polysaccharides. These constituents underpin moisturising, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant behaviour when applied topically or formulated into ingestible preparations.

Nutritional value

  • Vitamins: notable levels of fat-soluble antioxidants (A, C, E).
  • Minerals: calcium, magnesium, zinc, and selenium support electrolyte balance.
  • Polysaccharides: complex carbohydrates that contribute to immune modulation.

Therapeutic properties

  • Deep hydration and barrier support for compromised skin.
  • Anti-inflammatory action that mitigates psoriasis, eczema, and acne flare-ups.
  • Antioxidant reservoir protecting tissue from oxidative stress.
  • Immune-enhancing polysaccharides and gentle digestive support when consumed responsibly.
  • Antimicrobial traits targeting select bacterial and fungal burdens.

Side effects and cautions

  • Potential for contact dermatitis in sensitised individuals.
  • Oral overuse may trigger diarrhoea, cramping, or electrolyte imbalance.
  • Allergens overlap with other Liliaceae members (onion, garlic, tulip).
