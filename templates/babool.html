Babool (Vachellia nilotica)

The Leguminosae family includes babool, also known as Acacia arabica (Vachellia nilotica). A perennial tree with spiny leaves that is typically found in India is called a babool. Babur or Babul is the Hindi name for the tree's bark. Because it was imported in significant quantities in Arab countries, it is now widely known as gum arabic.

In India, the dry regions of Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Rajasthan, and other states are very common places to find babool. Additionally, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and Burma, three neighbouring Asian nations, are home to the prickly babool tree. It is indigenous to North Africa and is frequently spotted in Egypt. The western states of the peninsula have a higher prevalence of it.

The common names for babool include Babura, Kikar, and Babula in Hindi, Kaloabaval, and Babul in Gujarati, Babala, Babul, and Babhul in Oriya, Babla in Bengali, and Babbala in Assamese, as well as the English names Babula tree and Indian gum Arabic tree. Kannada names for prisons include Kari Jail, Kari Gobli, Sharmeeruka, and Pulai Jali; Punjabi names include Kikkar; and Kashmiri names include Sak. Telugu has Thumma, Nallatumma, Tamil has Karuvel, Karuvelan, Malayalam has Velutha Karuvelan, and Sanskrit has Bavari, Kinkirata.



Nutritional Value of Babool :


  • Babool seeds are a great source of protein and fibre. Compared to the leaves, its pods are higher in fibre and lower in protein.

  • Babool contains tannin, alkaloids, a polyphenolic compound, terpenoids, glycosides, and saponins, among other beneficial nutrients.


Properties of Babool :


  • It might possess antibacterial, antifungal, or antiviral properties.

  • It may have properties that reduce blood pressure, blood sugar, or spasms.

  • It might have demulcent or astringent properties that could reduce pain.

  • It might be advantageous for the stomach.

  • It might have anti-diarrheal, anti-malarial, haemostatic (may stop bleeding), or anti-inflammatory properties.

  • It might have immunomodulatory, anti-thrombotic, anti-ulcer, anti-cancer, or anti-helminthic activity (which could prevent blood clots).


Side Effects of Babool :


    Babool may have some stomach-related side effects if consumed frequently.
  • If used improperly, babool could harm the chest, rectum, and intestine in addition to possibly causing constipation.

  • When breastfeeding or pregnant, stay away from babool.

  • If you experience any of these side effects, seek immediate medical attention from the doctor who prescribed the medication to you. To help you deal with the side effects, they will prescribe you the best treatment they can.


Interactions with other Drugs :


According to a review article (Ulbricht et al. 2008), babool and the antibiotic amoxicillin were found to interact. Amoxicillin's absorption might be impacted. So, stay away from babool if you're taking amoxicillin.
