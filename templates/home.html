How scientists use MPMDB

Structured around a reproducible discovery workflow so your next grant, publication, or formulation can cite traceable evidence.

  1. 01 · Contextualize

    Start with taxonomy + botanical overview

    Establish nomenclature and validated morphology with herbarium imagery to avoid sample drift.

  2. 02 · Decode

    Compare genomics and transcriptomics layers

    Spot biosynthetic pathway genes, expression spikes, and SNPs impacting secondary metabolite production.

  3. 03 · Prioritize

    Correlate omics with metabolite evidence

    Align proteomic abundances with LC-MS/MS signatures and literature-backed bioactivity.

  4. 04 · Communicate

    Share annotated dossiers

    Export curated summaries or cite permanent URLs inside manuscripts, dossiers, and regulatory filings.

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers for new collaborators and visiting scientist teams.

What metabolites are indexed?

Alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolics, glycosides, and essential oils with evidence tags.

Is the database expanding?

Yes. Quarterly release cycles bring new plants, omics layers, and FAIR data exports.

Can I contribute data?

Submit via our curatorial form. Each submission is peer reviewed before publication.
