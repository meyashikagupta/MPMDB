Peppermint (Mentha piperita)

Peppermint, scientifically known as Mentha piperita, is a popular herb that is valued for its refreshing aroma, cooling sensation, and potential health benefits. Peppermint oil, when diluted and applied topically, can provide a cooling and soothing effect on the skin. It may help relieve itching, irritation, and minor skin discomfort.Peppermint oil, in particular, has shown to have relaxant effects on the muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, which may promote smoother digestion.



Nutritional Value of Peppermint :


    The nutrients found are:

  • Calories:70

  • Carbohydrates:14 grams

  • Protein:3 grams

  • Fat:1 gram

  • Vitamin A:57% of the recommended daily intake (RDI)

  • Vitamin C:70% of the RDI

  • Calcium:20% of the RDI

  • Iron:55% of the RDI

  • Potassium:7% of the RDI


Properties of Peppermint :


  • Antispasmodic:Peppermint has antispasmodic properties, meaning it can help relax smooth muscles in the body.

  • Carminative:Peppermint is known for its carminative properties, which means it can help alleviate gas and bloating by aiding in the release of trapped gas from the digestive system.

  • Anti-nausea:Peppermint has been traditionally used to help alleviate nausea and motion sickness. The aroma of peppermint, in particular, can have a calming effect on the stomach and reduce feelings of nausea.

  • Mild Pain Relief:The cooling effect of peppermint, along with its analgesic properties, can offer mild pain relief. It may help soothe headaches, muscle aches, and minor discomforts when applied topically or used in aromatherapy.

  • Respiratory Support:Inhaling the vapors of peppermint or consuming peppermint tea may help open up the airways and provide relief from respiratory congestion, coughs, and sinus congestion.


Side Effects of Peppermint :


  • Gastroesophageal Reflux (GERD):Peppermint may relax the lower esophageal sphincter (LES), the muscle that separates the stomach from the esophagus, which can lead to the reflux of stomach acid and exacerbate symptoms of GERD in susceptible individuals.

  • Skin Sensitivity:Direct contact with undiluted peppermint oil can cause skin irritation, especially in individuals with sensitive skin.

  • Heartburn or Acid Reflux:Peppermint can sometimes cause a burning sensation or worsening of heartburn symptoms in individuals with sensitive digestive systems.

  • Interaction with Gallstones:Peppermint oil may potentially worsen symptoms or trigger spasms in individuals with gallstones or gallbladder disorders


Interactions with other Drugs :


    It is essential to take precautions when using Peppermint with:

  • Medications for Acid Reflux (Proton Pump Inhibitors):Peppermint may reduce the effectiveness of proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) used to treat acid reflux by relaxing the lower esophageal sphincter (LES).

  • Antacids:Peppermint may decrease the effectiveness of antacids by promoting the rapid emptying of the stomach.

  • Cyclosporine:Peppermint may increase the absorption of cyclosporine, a medication used to prevent organ rejection after transplantation.
