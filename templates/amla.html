Amla (Phyllanthus emblica)

Amla, also known as Indian gooseberry or Emblica officinalis, is a fruit that is highly regarded in Ayurveda for its medicinal properties. It is known for its high nutritional content and is considered a rich source of antioxidants.

Amla, Amalaki or Indian Gooseberry is a highly potent medicinal plant for its innumerable healing properties. Amalaki, as it is popularly known in Sanskrit and in ancient texts of Ayurveda, has various meanings – mother, nurse, immortality and it also means 'sour'.

Amla is a powerhouse of antioxidants and ancient medicine endorses the consumption of this fruit to prevent the formation of cancer cells. It can be consumed raw, in the form of juice, churna, candy, pickles or supplements.



Nutritional Value of Amla :


    The nutrients found are:

  • Calories:44

  • Iron:0.3 milligrams (2% of the recommended daily intake)

  • Vitamin C:27.7 milligrams (46% of the recommended daily intake)

  • Vitamin A:290 International Units (6% of the recommended daily intake)

  • Protein:0.9 grams


Properties of Amla :


  • Antioxidant:Amla is known for its exceptional antioxidant properties. It contains a high concentration of vitamin C and other antioxidants like flavonoids and polyphenols.

  • Immune-Boosting:Amla is renowned for its immune-boosting properties. The high vitamin C content stimulates the production of white blood cells, enhances immune response, and strengthens the immune system.

  • Anti-Inflammatory:Amla possesses potent anti-inflammatory properties. It contains bioactive compounds like gallic acid, ellagic acid, and quercetin, which help reduce inflammation in the body.

  • Digestive Health:Amla is known to support digestive health. It acts as a natural digestive tonic, helps improve digestion, and relieves gastrointestinal issues like indigestion, constipation, and acidity.

  • Cardiovascular Health:Amla is believed to have cardiovascular benefits. It may help lower cholesterol levels, reduce blood pressure, and improve overall heart health. The antioxidants present in amla protect against oxidative stress and help maintain healthy blood vessels.

  • Liver Protection:Amla has hepatoprotective properties, meaning it helps protect the liver from damage. It supports liver function, promotes detoxification, and helps maintain liver health.


Side Effects of Amla :


  • Diarrhea:Excessive consumption of amla or amla products like amla juice or supplements may lead to loose stools or diarrhea.

  • Allergic Reactions:Although rare, some individuals may be allergic to amla. Allergic reactions to amla can manifest as skin rashes, itching, swelling, or difficulty breathing

  • Amla is highly acidic, and consuming it in large amounts or on an empty stomach may cause stomach discomfort, acidity, or gastrointestinal upset


Interactions with other Drugs :


    It is essential to take precautions when using Amla with:

  • Anticoagulant/Antiplatelet Drugs: Amla contains vitamin C and other components that may have mild blood-thinning effects. Combining amla with anticoagulant or antiplatelet medications, such as warfarin, aspirin, or clopidogrel, may increase the risk of bleeding.

  • Antidiabetic Medications:Amla may lower blood sugar levels. When combined with antidiabetic medications like insulin or oral hypoglycemic drugs, it can further lower blood sugar levels and potentially cause hypoglycemia (low blood sugar).

  • Antacids and H2 Blockers: Amla is acidic and may interfere with the absorption of certain antacids and H2 blocker medications.
