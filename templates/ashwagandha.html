Botanical synopsis

Withania somnifera is a small shrub native to arid South Asia, Africa, and Central Asia. Over fifty distinct chemical entities have been isolated across leaves, roots, berries, and seeds, fueling its long-standing use as a calming nervine tonic and restorative agent.

Core properties

  • Sleep-promoting and analgesic behaviour helpful in stress-linked insomnia.
  • Mild diuretic and astringent effects supporting detoxification.
  • Antihelminthic activity against common parasites.
  • Hypoglycaemic and depurative tendencies aiding metabolic balance.
  • Thyroprotective evidence safeguarding endocrine resilience.

Side effects

  • Rare hepatotoxicity has been reportedâ€”monitor liver enzymes during prolonged use.
  • Common GI reactions include nausea, diarrhoea, vomiting, or stomach upset at high doses.
  • Less frequent responses: drowsiness, dizziness, cough, rash, blurred vision, weight gain, hallucinations.

Drug interactions

  • Barbiturates: potentiation of sedative effects requires clinician oversight.
  • Alcohol: concurrent use amplifies CNS depression and is discouraged.
  • Sedatives: additive somnolence expected with benzodiazepines or antihistamines.

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)

Withania somnifera, also known as ashwagandha, is a common herb used in Ayurvedic medicine. It is a small shrub that is a member of the Solanaceae family. It might be helpful for a variety of illnesses and primarily as a nervine tonic (has a calming effect on nerves). Ashwagandha is also known as Indian winter cherry or Indian ginseng. The rasayana (tonic) property of ashwagandha is well known. Rasayana is a herbal or metallic concoction that promotes happiness and a youthful state of physical and mental health.

In dry parts of South Asia, Africa, and Central Asia, ashwagandha is grown. A total of more than 50 chemical components have been isolated from various ashwagandha plant parts.



Nutritional Value of Ashwagandha :


    In 100 grams of ashwagandha, the nutrients found are:

  • Energy ---> 250g

  • Total dietary fibre ---> 25g

  • carbohydrate ---> 75g


Properties of Ashwagandha :


  • It might aid in promoting sleep and pain relief.

  • It might cause the body to expel urine in a diuretic manner.

  • It might have an astringent (tissue-constricting) effect.

  • It might function as an antihelminthic (against worm parasites).

  • It might have hypoglycemic (blood sugar lowering) and depurative (detoxifying) properties.

  • It might protect the thyroid gland (be thyroprotective).


Side Effects of Ashwagandha :


  • Ashwagandha may harm the liver.

  • Ashwagandha's most frequent side effects include nausea, diarrhoea, vomiting, and stomach upset.

  • Drosiness, vertigo (dizziness), cough and congestion, rashes, blurred vision, dry mouth, weight gain, and hallucinations are some of the less frequent side effects.


Interactions with other Drugs :


    It is essential to take precautions when using ashwagandha with:

  • Barbiturates (a group of drugs that cause relaxation or sleepiness):Ashwagandha may intensify the effects of a class of drugs known as barbiturates, which promote relaxation or sleepiness. So, when taking this combination, care must be taken.

  • Alcohol:Ashwagandha shouldn't be taken with alcohol.

  • Sedatives:Ashwagandha shouldn't be consumed with medicines that have sedative properties.
