Clove(Syzygium aromaticum)

Clove, also known as Syzygium aromaticum, is a spice derived from the dried flower buds of the clove tree. It is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine due to its aromatic flavor and potential health benefits. They are particularly rich in dietary fiber, manganese, vitamin C, and several minerals. They also contain smaller amounts of other vitamins and minerals.



Nutritional Value of Clove :


    The nutrients found are:

  • Calories:323 kcal

  • Carbohydrates:61 grams

  • Protein:6 grams

  • Fat:13 grams

  • Fiber:34 grams

  • Vitamin C:80 milligrams

  • Vitamin K:14.8 micrograms

  • Vitamin E:6 milligrams

  • Calcium:631 milligrams


Properties of Clove :


  • Aromatic Flavor:Cloves have a strong, warm, and aromatic flavor that adds depth and richness to various dishes.

  • Anti-Inflammatory Effects:Cloves possess anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the body.

  • Nalgesic and Anesthetic Properties:Clove oil, derived from cloves, contains eugenol, which exhibits analgesic (pain-relieving) and anesthetic properties.

  • Anti-Oxidant Activity:Cloves are rich in antioxidants, which help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals. The antioxidants in cloves may help reduce oxidative stress and have potential anti-aging and disease-preventive effects.


Side Effects of Clove :


  • Allergic Reactions:Some individuals may have allergies or sensitivities to cloves. Allergic reactions can include symptoms such as skin rash, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing, or gastrointestinal distress.

  • Blood Thinning Effects:Cloves contain eugenol, a compound that may have mild blood-thinning properties.

  • Dental Sensitivity:Clove oil is sometimes used for toothache relief due to its numbing properties. However, excessive use or direct application of undiluted clove oil to the gums or teeth may cause irritation, sensitivity, or damage to the oral tissues.

  • Gastro-Intestinal Issues:In high doses, cloves may cause digestive discomfort, including heartburn, stomach upset, or diarrhea.


Interactions with other Drugs :


    It is essential to take precautions when using Clove with:

  • Liver-Metabolized Drugs:Clove oil or supplements may interact with medications that are metabolized by the liver, such as certain antiviral drugs, antifungal medications, and some antidepressants.

  • Diabetes Medications:Clove supplements or concentrated forms may have potential effects on blood sugar levels.
