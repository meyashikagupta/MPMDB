Bhringaraj (Eclipta prostrata)

Bhringaraj, also known as "Eclipta alba" or "False Daisy," is a plant that is commonly used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. It is native to India and has been used for various therapeutic purposes.

Bhringaraj is rich in phytochemicals such as flavonoids, alkaloids, triterpenes, and polyphenols. These compounds are believed to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and hepatoprotective properties.



Nutritional value of Bhringaraj :


    The nutrients found are:

  • Vitamins:It contain vitamins such as vitamin C and vitamin E.

  • Minerals:It is rich in minerals like calcium, iron, magnesium, and phosphorus.


Properties of Bhringaraj :


  • Liver Health:Bhringaraj is known for its hepatoprotective properties, meaning it may help protect and support liver health.

  • Hair Care:Bhringaraj is widely used in Ayurvedic hair care preparations due to its potential benefits for hair health. It is believed to promote hair growth, strengthen hair follicles, and help prevent hair fall.

  • Anti-Inflammatory:Bhringaraj has anti-inflammatory properties, which may help in managing various inflammatory conditions.

  • Anti-Oxidant:Bhringaraj contains compounds that act as antioxidants which help neutralize harmful free radicals in the body, which can protect cells from oxidative stress and potential damage.

  • Skin Care:Bhringaraj is used in traditional Ayurvedic skincare to treat various skin conditions and believed to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that may help in managing skin disorders such as eczema, dermatitis, and psoriasis.

  • Respiratory Health:Bhringaraj is sometimes used in Ayurveda for respiratory issues like asthma and bronchitis.


Side Effects of Bhringaraj :


  • Allergic Reactions:Some individuals may be allergic to bhringaraj. If you have a known allergy to plants in the Asteraceae family , there is a possibility of being allergic to bhringaraj as well.

  • Skin Irritation:When used topically, bhringaraj preparations may cause skin irritation or contact dermatitis in some individuals.

  • Digestive Discomfort:In some cases, internal use of bhringaraj preparations, especially in high doses, may cause digestive discomfort such as nausea, stomach upset, or diarrhea.


Interactions with other Drugs :


    It is essential to take precautions when using Bhringaraj with:

  • Anticoagulant/Antiplatelet Drugs:Bhringaraj may possess potential anticoagulant properties. Therefore, there is a theoretical risk of interaction with anticoagulant or antiplatelet medications such as warfarin, heparin, aspirin, or clopidogrel.

  • Immunosuppressive Drugs:Bhringaraj may modulate the immune system. If you are taking immunosuppressive medications, such as corticosteroids or medications used for autoimmune diseases, there is a possibility of interaction.
